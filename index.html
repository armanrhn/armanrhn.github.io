Who Am I?

I am a PhD researcher in Operations Research (OR). My research focuses on the design and analysis of optimization algorithms for challenging scheduling and allocation problems motivated by real-world applications. I am particularly interested in applied optimization problems such as resource allocation, supply chain management, and transportation. I enjoy working on mathematical models that capture the structure of these complex problems and on turning them into solutions that can be implemented and maintained. I have hands-on experience developing software and data pipelines that support optimization models and make them useful in practice. I hold an MSc in Computer Science and a BSc in Information Technology Engineering, where I developed a strong foundation in theoretical and applied computational methods. Additionally, I bring over four years of academic research experience. Alongside research, I am currently working as a Lecturer at Maastricht University , and I like explaining complex quantitative ideas in a way that is clear, practical, and useful for different audiences.

Projects


  • Measuring the Inefficiency of Decentralized Scheduling on Related Machines

    In this project we apply the primal-dual technique to bound the price of anarchy of a scheduling game on related machines.

    Advisors: Dr. Andre Berger , Dr. Marc Schröder

  • Fixed Order Scheduling with Deadlines

    In this project we develop exact and approximation algorithms for scheduling jobs with an imposed processing order on the minimum possible number of machines.

    Advisors: Dr. Andre Berger , Dr. Marc Schröder

  • Cost Preserving Dependent Rounding for Allocation Problems

    In this project we provide a dependent randomized rounding algorithm for many-to-many assignments on a bipartite graph that satisfies cost-preservation properties.

    Advisor: Dr. Lars Rohwedder

  • Coordinate-Wise Median Mechanism for Facility Location Problem

    In this project we improve the approximation guarantee of the coordinate-wise median mechanism for the facility location problem.

    working paper

    Advisor: Dr. Andre Berger

MSc in Computer Science (2018-2021)

Institute for Advanced Studies in Basic Sciences, Zanjan, Iran

Project


  • Path Planning with Objectives Minimum Length and Maximum Clearance

    In this project we provide an efficient algorithm for the bi-objective path planning problem that minimizes path length (Manhattan metric) while maximizing distance from obstacles (Euclidean metric), aiming to compute Pareto-optimal paths.

    Advisor: Dr. Mansoor Davoodi Monfared

BSc in Information Technology Engineering (2014-2018)

Institute for Advanced Studies in Basic Sciences, Zanjan, Iran

Project


  • Persian Word Sense Disambiguation Based on Expert Systems

    In this project we develop a word sense disambiguation system for the Persian language using expert systems. The result is used in AvaNama application that converts speech/text to Persian Sign Language.

    Advisor: Dr. Mansoor Davoodi Monfared
